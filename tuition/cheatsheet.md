# Heads-up NLHE cheat sheet (condensed tuition)

Dense, high-signal strategy maxims for heads-up No-Limit Hold'em (~100bb, small
blind = button). Apply these on every decision; keep your reply short.

## Preflop
- You only face ONE opponent and post a blind every hand, so play WIDE and aggressive. Folding the button bleeds chips.
- Button (small blind): open-raise to ~2-2.5bb with any pair, any ace, any two cards ten+, most suited connectors. Open ~60-80% of hands.
- Big blind: defend wide vs a single raise — call or 3-bet rather than fold. Folding too much is the #1 leak.
- 3-bet premiums (TT+, AQ+) and some bluffs; 4-bet only the very top.

## Pot odds
- Call if equity > pot odds. Pot odds = call / (pot + call). Calling 2 into a 6 pot needs ~25% equity.
- Outs to equity: outs ×2 per remaining card, ×4 on the flop for both cards. Flush draw ≈ 9 outs ≈ 36% by river.

## Position & aggression
- Acting last is a big edge — bet your good hands AND your draws (semi-bluff), barrel when you represent strength.
- As preflop raiser, c-bet most flops ~1/2 to 2/3 pot. Being the aggressor wins via fold equity + showdown equity.
- Passive calling loses; when unsure between call and raise with a strong hand or good draw, prefer raise.

## Bet sizing (fractions of the pot)
- Value/bluff small (1/3-1/2) on dry boards, larger (2/3-pot) on wet boards and for big draws.
- Size value bets and bluffs the SAME so you're not readable. Overbet only with the nuts or as a polar bluff.

## Postflop
- Top pair+ is usually best heads-up — bet it for value across streets; don't slow-play good-but-vulnerable hands.
- With strong draws, semi-bluff. With weak made hands, control the pot (check/call), don't bloat it.
- Give up cheaply when you have no equity and face strength; don't pay off obvious value.

## Exploits
- Vs a passive/calling opponent: value bet relentlessly, stop bluffing.
- Vs an over-aggressive opponent: trap, call down lighter, let them barrel into your strong hands.

## Quick rules
- Raise or fold more than you call; limping and flat-calling are usually weak.
- Be the aggressor; bet good hands and good draws.
- Defend your big blind wide; don't fold your way to zero.
- Use position to apply pressure and realise equity.
- Equity vs pot odds decides every call.
- Keep value and bluff sizing identical.
- Punish passivity; trap aggression.
