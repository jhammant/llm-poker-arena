# Preflop hand selection and ranges for heads-up NLHE

This primer covers preflop play for heads-up (1v1) No-Limit Hold'em at ~100bb effective stacks. The small blind acts first preflop and **is the button** (acts last postflop). All sizings are in big blinds (bb) or pot fractions.

## Why heads-up ranges are much wider

In full-ring you fight 8 opponents for one pot, so you fold weak hands and wait for premiums. Heads-up you face exactly one opponent and you are forced to post a blind every hand. Two facts drive everything:

- **You only need to beat one random hand.** Any hand has ~50% raw equity against a random hand. Even a weak holding like Q5 is a favorite over a truly random hand.
- **Blinds are constant pressure.** Folding the small blind surrenders 0.5bb every time; folding the big blind to a raise surrenders even more. Tight play bleeds chips.

Result: the button (SB) open-raises a huge fraction of hands, and the big blind defends very wide. Ranges that would be reckless 9-handed are standard here.

## Hand groupings

| Class | Example hands | Heads-up action |
|-------|--------------|-----------------|
| Premium | AA-TT, AK, AQ | Always raise; happy to get stacks in |
| Strong | 99-77, AJ-AT, KQ, KJ, QJs | Raise; 3-bet and stack off readily |
| Playable | Any pair, any ace, K9+, Q9+, J9+, suited connectors 54s+ | Standard button open; defend BB |
| Marginal | Offsuit gappers (T7o, 96o), weak suited (Q4s, J3s), K2o-K6o | Open most as button; defend or fold in BB |
| Trash | Offsuit hands with a gap and no high card (72o, 83o, 94o) | Fold even the button when in doubt |

"s" = suited, "o" = offsuit. Suitedness adds ~3-4% equity and flush playability, so suited hands jump up a class.

## Opening from the button (small blind)

The button is the most profitable seat: you act last on every postflop street. **Open-raise to 2-2.5bb** with a very wide range, or limp/min-raise as a mixed strategy. A strong default open includes:

- **Any pair** (22+)
- **Any ace** (A2+, suited or offsuit)
- **Any two cards 9 or higher** (T9, J9, Q9, K9, plus all face combos)
- **Any king** suited, most kings offsuit
- **Most suited connectors and one-gappers** (54s, 65s, 75s, 86s, etc.)

That is roughly 70-85% of all hands. Fold only true trash: offsuit hands with a big gap and no high card (72o, 84o, 93o, J2o-J3o). When unsure on the button, raise — the positional edge plus fold equity makes marginal opens profitable.

## Defending the big blind

You already have 1bb invested and you close the action, so you get excellent pot odds. Against a 2-2.5bb open you risk ~1-1.5bb to win ~3-4bb, so you only need ~25-30% equity to continue. **Defend 55-70% of hands.**

- **Call (flat)** with playable-to-marginal hands that flop well: suited aces, suited connectors, broadways, small/medium pairs, any two cards T+.
- **3-bet (re-raise to ~3x the open, e.g. 7-9bb)** with a polarized range: premiums for value (TT+, AQ+, AK) plus light bluffs that play poorly as calls (A2s-A5s, K9s, suited one-gappers, J5s-type blockers).
- **Fold** only the weakest offsuit junk (82o, 73o, 94o) that misses too often to defend profitably.

Never fold a hand with a pair, an ace, or two broadway cards to a single raise in the BB.

## 3-bet and 4-bet ranges

**3-betting (BB vs SB open, or SB re-raising a limp):** Use a polarized structure — strong value hands plus the lowest-equity bluffs that have blocker value. Size to ~3x the raise out of position, ~2.5x in position. Value: TT+, AQ+, AK. Bluffs: weak suited aces (A2s-A5s, blockers to AA/AK), suited kings (K7s-K9s), suited gappers you'd otherwise fold or call thinly.

**Facing a 3-bet (4-bet decision):** 4-bet to ~2.2-2.5x the 3-bet. **4-bet for value** with QQ+, AK, and sometimes AQs/JJ. **4-bet bluff** with hands that block strong continues — A5s, A4s, KQs — then fold to a 5-bet/shove unless you hold AA/KK. Call the 3-bet (rather than 4-bet) with hands strong enough to play a big pot but that prefer to keep bluffs in: JJ-99, AQ, AJs, KQs.

**Stacking off:** At 100bb, getting it all in preflop is correct only with QQ+/AK (and KK+/AK against tight opponents). Against an unknown, do not stack off JJ or AQ preflop.

## Quick rules

- On the button, raise any pair, any ace, any two cards 9+, and most suited connectors — fold only gappy offsuit junk.
- In the big blind, defend 55-70%; never fold a pair, an ace, or two broadways to one raise.
- Suited beats offsuit — suitedness bumps a hand up roughly one class.
- 3-bet polarized: premiums for value plus low-equity suited blockers as bluffs.
- 4-bet for value with QQ+/AK; 4-bet-bluff with A5s/A4s/KQs and fold to the shove.
- Get 100bb in preflop only with QQ+ and AK.
- When in doubt on the button, raise; when in doubt in the BB facing one raise, call.
