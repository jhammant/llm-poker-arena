# Postflop play: board texture, made hands and draws

Heads-up NLHE, ~100bb effective, small blind = button. This is decision tuition: read the board, classify your hand, pick a sizing.

## Reading board texture

**Dry boards** have few draws and isolated ranks: K72 rainbow, Q83r, A92r. Equity is "sticky" — whoever has top pair is rarely overtaken. Bet small (25-33% pot) and often, because little can call or draw out.

**Wet boards** are connected and/or two-tone: 987, JT8ss, 765hh, KQ9. Many turn/river cards change the winner. Bet bigger (60-75% pot) with strong hands and strong draws; check more middling hands because they get raised off equity.

Score a flop fast: count (a) two cards that allow flush draws, (b) connectedness within 4 ranks, (c) presence of an ace/broadway. 2+ of these = wet, play polarized and larger.

## Made hands

**Top pair**: a value hand, not a monster. On dry boards bet 2-3 streets for thin value. On wet boards bet flop, then reassess — top pair with a weak kicker is a pot-control hand on the turn (check back / call rather than bet-bet-bet). Fold top-pair-weak-kicker to large turn+river aggression on coordinated boards.

**Two pair / sets**: bet for value on almost every texture. On wet boards prefer larger sizing (65-80%) to charge draws and build a pot for stacks-in by the river. With a set on a dry board, you can occasionally check the flop to induce, but defaulting to betting is fine and rarely wrong. Sets are your stack-off hands.

**Overpairs**: strong but vulnerable. On dry boards, three streets of value. On wet boards, size up early; be willing to pot-control or even fold to a check-raise + barrel when the runout completes obvious draws and the opponent is not bluff-heavy.

## Draws and semi-bluffing

A flush draw is ~9 outs (~35% to complete by the river, ~19% on the next card). An open-ended straight draw is 8 outs (~32% / ~17%). A combo draw (flush + straight, 12-15 outs) is often a favorite vs one pair — play it fast and big.

**Semi-bluff** strong draws (8+ clean outs) by betting/raising: you win now via folds, or improve later. Prefer raising draws on the flop when you have overcards too — extra equity and extra fold equity. Bare gutshots (4 outs) are bluffs, not semi-bluffs; use them as occasional barrels, not stack-off hands.

## Turn decisions

The turn defines the hand. Ask: did my equity go up or down, and did the board get scarier?

- Bet big (66-100%) when you have value on a wet board, or a strong draw you want to deny equity with.
- Slow down (check) thin made hands (middle pair, weak top pair) — you want a cheap showdown.
- A turn card that completes a draw and your opponent leads or check-raises is a real signal; respect it without a strong hand.

## River decisions

No more equity to realize — every bet is value or bluff.

- **Value bet thin** when a worse hand can call: bet 50-66% with top pair good kicker on a dry runout, because second pair / weak top pair will pay. Size up on wet runouts where you are polarized.
- **Bluff** missed draws that block your opponent's calls and unblock their folds; choose hands with no showdown value (busted flush > a small pair you can show down).
- **Give up** when your busted hand still beats some bluff-catchers — check and take the showdown rather than turning a winner into a bluff.

## Pot control

Keep the pot small with medium-strength hands (one pair, decent kicker) so a single mistake does not cost stacks. Check back the turn to realize equity cheaply and bluff-catch one river bet. Conversely, build the pot early when you hold the nuts-region so the stacks are in by the river. As the SB/button you control sizing and can check back to cap the pot in position — use that edge often with marginal made hands.

## Quick rules

- 2+ of {flush draw, connected, broadway/ace} = wet: bet bigger and more polarized.
- Sets and two pair stack off; top pair is 1-2 streets, not 3 on wet boards.
- Semi-bluff 8+ out draws aggressively; bare gutshots are bluffs, not calls.
- On wet boards charge draws 65-80%; on dry boards bet small 25-33% and often.
- River: value bet only if a worse hand calls; otherwise check the bluff-catcher.
- Bluff with busted draws that block calls; give up made hands that beat bluffs.
- Pot-control medium hands in position by checking back the turn.
